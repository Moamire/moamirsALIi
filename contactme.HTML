Event recap / March 2025

Puntland Technology Expo
2025 Edition

Showcasing innovation, youth-driven tech, and a vision to solve unemployment in Somalia.

📅 JAN 5-8, 2026 📍 Garowe, Puntland 🎤 Presenter & Innovator

Every year, the Puntland Technology Expo brings together the brightest minds — scientists, economists, university leaders, and tech pioneers — to address real societal challenges. This year, I had the honor of being one of the featured presenters, representing a new wave of developers building solutions for Somalia's most urgent problems.

Presenting a vision to fight unemployment

My presentation focused on a job-tech ecosystem designed to bridge the gap between employers, freelancers, and volunteers across Puntland and Somalia. The audience — including government officials, tech leaders, and university deans — responded with overwhelming enthusiasm.

The core idea: a centralized platform where job seekers, volunteers, and local businesses connect seamlessly — reducing unemployment by making opportunities visible and accessible. The expo became the perfect stage to demonstrate the main prototype and gather real-time feedback.

🎤 Delivering the keynote presentation — “Tech for Employment”
📱 Live demo of the job & volunteer matching platform
🔍 The app demo

JobConnect — a hyperlocal solution

I walked the audience through the JobConnect prototype — a mobile-first platform where users can browse job listings, post volunteer opportunities, and connect with employers in their region. The live demo highlighted the main dashboard, job filtering by distance, and an intuitive application flow.

The feedback was electric: industry leaders and young developers approached me after the session, sharing how this could reshape the local job market. Many praised the idea of integrating volunteer work as a pathway to employment — a fresh angle that resonated deeply.

A gathering of change-makers

The expo wasn't just about presentations — it was a vibrant convergence of brilliant minds. From university researchers showcasing agricultural AI to economists debating fiscal policy, the energy was inspiring. Hundreds of attendees, including students, entrepreneurs, and international partners, filled the hall.

Being part of this ecosystem reminded me why technology matters most when it serves the community. I exchanged ideas with other developers, received mentorship offers, and connected with potential collaborators who believe in the mission to reduce unemployment through digital tools.

What's next for JobConnect?

I'm actively developing the full-stack version of the platform using React Native, Supabase, and real-time features. The expo gave me crucial validation and beta testers — expect a limited pilot launch in Q3 2025 across major cities in Puntland.

Beyond the expo

I left the event with new partnerships, an expanded network, and a reinforced drive to build tech that matters. Stay tuned for more updates, open-source contributions, and community workshops.
